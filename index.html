Police Accountability Board

The City of Rochester does not hold police officers accountable for misconduct and brutality. Between 2001 and 2016 there were 929 allegations of excessive force filed by citizens. The Chief of Police, who has the sole power to discipline officers, only sustained 16 of these allegations. This is an astounding miscarriage of justice. And the reason is obvious: the Chief of Police cannot be expected to investigate, judge, and discipline police officers. This is a clear conflict of interest. Even if the Chief does choose to sustain complaints against officers, rarely does the punishment amount to anything substantial. Between 2002 and 2016, only 13 officers were disciplined, of those only 6 were suspended, and none were fired. Police misconduct will not change until this broken system in replaced with an independent system that serves the interest of the citizens of Rochester not the RPD.

The Police Accountability Board is a civilian led oversight body that is being proposed as a replacement to the current system. The PAB would be independent of the RPD, investigate cases, subpoena evidence and witnesses, conduct hearings, and have disciplinary authority.
